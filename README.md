## Overview

Hantu Bungkus ('wrapped ghost') is the Malaysian name for the figure widely known across the Malay-Indonesian world as Pocong. It is rooted in Islamic burial practice: a deceased Muslim is washed, then wrapped in a kain kafan (white burial shroud) tied at the head, neck, waist, and feet. According to folk tradition, if the knots are not loosened in the grave after burial, the soul cannot depart and the body becomes a hantu bungkus / pocong, an animate, shroud-bound revenant. Because its feet are bound together, the creature is unable to walk and is depicted as moving by hopping, often in a manner compared to the Chinese jiangshi; some traditions hold that authentic pocong actually float rather than hop, and that hopping in folk depictions is an embellishment. Encounters are reported as terrifying but typically not directly lethal — pocong are usually said to startle or warn rather than physically harm. The figure is one of the most recognizable in Malay-Indonesian horror media, anchoring a long line of films, television series, and internet ghost stories from at least the early 2000s.

## Etymology

- **Origin**: Malay: hantu ('ghost') + bungkus ('wrapped, parcelled'). Indonesian/Javanese pocong is a separate term from the same semantic field, derived from Javanese for 'wrapped (corpse)'.
- **Meaning**: Literally 'wrapped ghost' — naming the entity after the unloosed Islamic burial shroud.
- **First use**: Term embedded in modern Malay/Indonesian usage; closely tied to Islamic burial customs that have been practiced in the region since at least the 13th century.

## Theories & Explanations

### Paranormal / Cryptozoological

**Restless soul of the unloosed dead** _(Malay-Indonesian folk Islam)_

Within the folk-religious frame, a Muslim whose shroud knots remain bound after burial is unable to depart, becoming a hantu bungkus until the knots are released.

### Skeptical

**Hoaxes and pranks** _(Malaysian and Indonesian press reporting)_

Many reported pocong sightings in Malaysia and Indonesia have been traced to pranksters wearing white sheets; police have arrested individuals on multiple occasions.

**Folkloric reinforcement of burial practice** _(Folklore studies of Malay-Islamic syncretism)_

The legend functions to enforce correct, respectful Islamic burial practice — particularly the proper untying of shroud knots — rather than to describe a literal entity.
